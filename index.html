Mahatma Gandhi

Biography

Mahatma Gandhi, born on October 2, 1869, was a prominent leader in India's struggle for independence from British rule. He is known for his philosophy of non-violence and civil disobedience.

Achievements

  • Played a key role in various civil rights movements.
  • Led the famous Salt March in 1930 to protest the British salt monopoly.
  • Advocated for social reforms and equality.

Legacy

Mahatma Gandhi's principles of non-violence and his dedication to social justice continue to inspire people worldwide. He is often referred to as the "Father of the Nation" in India.
